Greencity Website Decision TEST

Stay on Houzez (via agency) vs. Build Custom (Next.js + Admin + Backend)

Goal: Choose the option that best supports growth, performance, integrations, and long‑term ROI.

Learn More Jump to Budget

Executive Summary (TL;DR)

  • We can stabilize the current Houzez stack, but we’ll remain limited by plugins, data model, and performance at scale.
  • The agency proposal rebuilds on the same theme; good for visuals & short‑term fixes, but doesn’t change the underlying constraints.
  • Vitaliy’s proposal: A custom Next.js + headless CMS + API gives us speed, SEO control, and clean integrations (Monday/CRM/investor portal), with higher upfront work but lower long‑term friction.

Current State (What’s hurting us)

  • Random bugs, occasional crashes, “broken data” requiring DB interventions.
  • Heavy footprint (~160 GB media/plugins/backups) → slow admin & slow pages.
  • Elementor + many plugins → bloat and conflict risk.
  • No real SEO foundation, minimal content, inconsistent imagery/video.
  • Integrations (Monday, CRM, internal DB) are plugin‑limited.
  • Theme/vendor dependency: unclear ownership of long‑term maintenance; migration later could be expensive.
  • Operational overhead: complex builder UI raises risk of editor mistakes and regressions.
  • Security surface: many plugins → more updates, more breakage risk without strict QA.

Additionally (EN)

  • Built on a WordPress theme with many plugins (incl. Elementor); much of the functionality is unused yet loads assets — total footprint is ~160 GB (can be reduced dramatically via offloading/cleanup).
  • As the company grows, needed automations/integrations will expand; plugin‑based approaches are often limited for business‑specific logic.
  • The theme requires ongoing vendor support; ownership is unclear. If support lapses, a future migration could be costly.

Impact: Lost trust (errors/latency), poor rankings, slow iteration, high support burden.

Root Causes

  • Architecture: Monolithic WP + theme + builder; tight coupling of content, layout, and business logic.
  • Data governance: Inconsistent post types/fields, legacy content, media not offloaded/CDN‑optimized.
  • Performance: Render blocking builder assets, unoptimized queries, plugin overlap, oversized images.
  • Integrations: Reliant on generic plugins vs. purpose‑built services/APIs.

Option A: Agency Rebuild on Houzez

What you get

  • Reviews, Google Optimization, Awards.
  • Photography and marketing videos scheduled based on web design.
  • Migration of property data.
  • Website development (based on theme); launch + public launch.
  • Team training with pre‑recorded videos on navigating the site backend.
  • Begin local SEO for target state(s).
  • On‑going support for the WordPress website.
  • Content planning for new pages: Location pages, Blogs, Service pages.

Pros

  • Fastest path to a cleaner site.
  • Clear fixed scope & service.

Cons

  • Underlying theme/builder constraints remain.
  • Future integrations/automation still rely on plugins.

Option C: Custom Next.js + Admin + Backend

Architecture

  • Next.js (SSR/ISR) front‑end for speed & SEO control.
  • Headless CMS (e.g., Strapi, Sanity, Payload, or WordPress‑as‑headless) for content ops.
  • Custom API/DB for listings, communities, programs, and CRM/Monday sync.

Benefits

  • Faster website experience: Pages load almost instantly, creating a smoother experience for visitors.
  • Better visibility on Google: Built‑in SEO advantages help more people find us online.
  • Fits our needs, not just the theme: We’re free to organize data and features around how our business really works.
  • Easier to connect with our tools: Integrates directly with Monday.com, HubSpot, and our investor portal without workarounds.
  • Smarter marketing: Easier A/B testing and clear analytics to track what performs best.
  • MLS‑ready + map visuals: Import/sync MLS/RESO data and present interactive map‑based unit visuals for communities and listings.

What “Custom” Delivers (Roles Clarified)

Goal: Make clear who does what (Dev vs. UI/UX vs. Agency/Marketing).

Phase 1

Design system, sitemap, content model, CMS setup.

Owner: Dev Team + UI/UX Designer for design system + components.

Agency (optional): Brand guidance, legacy asset review.

Phase 2

Core pages (Home, Search Homes, Communities, Approach, Financing, Careers, Contact), reusable sections, forms.

Owner: Dev Team (implementation) + UI/UX Designer (UI layouts, component specs).

Agency: N/A (or visual QA only, if engaged).

Phase 3

Listings/communities admin, importers (CSV/MLS/RESO feed), Monday/CRM sync MVP.

Owner: Dev Team.

UI/UX Designer: Admin UX flows & empty/error states.

Agency: N/A.

Phase 4

Technical SEO/schema implementation, animations framework, QA, redirects, launch.

Owner: Dev Team (technical SEO only).

UI/UX Designer: Motion guidelines & micro‑interactions.

Agency/Marketing (optional): SEO campaign, launch comms.

Parallel (Agency/Marketing): Photo/video production and content writing.

Parallel (Dev Team): Integrate assets, wire into CMS, publish, ensure performance/analytics.

Risk Matrix & Mitigation

Risk A: Agency (Houzez) C: Custom
Regressions from plugins High → lock versions Low → minimal plugins
Editor complexity Medium (Elementor) Low (components)
Integration limits High (plugin constraints) Low (first‑class APIs)
Launch delay Low Medium → staged rollout

Mitigation for C: freeze scope, deliver MVP first, parallel content work, keep legacy site live until cutover.
